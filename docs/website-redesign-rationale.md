# Website Redesign Rationale

**Objective:** the public site is the first artifact an investor or institution evaluates.
Every decision below exists to make it read as mission-critical financial infrastructure —
Aladdin's institutional weight with Apple's precision — not a startup dashboard.

**Carbon surfaces, no shadows.** Elevation is expressed as a surface step
(`#050505 → #171717`) separated by 1px hairlines, the way trading terminals and risk systems
actually build density. Shadows and glass imply consumer software; layered graphite implies
hardware. The old paper-white marketing skin, glassmorphism panels and radial-glow hero were
removed entirely.

**One fixed voice, ten routes.** Every public page opens with the same construction (indexed
rule, mono eyebrow, display headline) and every panel uses the same frame. Institutions trust
systems that behave identically everywhere; variety reads as improvisation.

**Type as instrumentation.** IBM Plex Sans at weight 600 with tight tracking for headers —
large but understated. IBM Plex Mono for every label and every numeral, with tabular figures
and aligned decimals, so data looks engineered rather than typeset. The editorial serif was
dropped: serifs say publication, grotesks say infrastructure.

**Color is information, never decoration.** The site is monochrome except where color carries
meaning: Bloomberg amber strictly for live/active market data, muted green/red strictly for
gains/losses, deep gold reserved (and deliberately unused). A visitor who sees amber knows
something is live; that discipline is itself a credibility signal. Blues, purples, gradients
and neon are banned.

**The hero is a control room, not a poster.** The screenshot-in-a-fake-browser-frame (which
overlapped the next section) was replaced by a coded operating picture: market tape, positions
table, VaR/CVaR surface and a Monte Carlo percentile fan rendered by the same component
grammar as the product, sitting in normal document flow. Showing the instrument beats
describing it. A dense cross-asset intelligence table follows for the same reason.

**Motion is nearly invisible.** 150–250ms, ease-out only: rows brighten, panels step up one
surface, content fades in 4px. The ping dots, "All systems operational" pulses, breathing
loaders and hover-scale effects were removed — systems that are actually critical do not
perform their own reliability.

**Copy states, never hypes.** Status lines were rewritten as facts (UTC clock, engine counts,
data windows), and rhetorical flourishes were cut. Quiet declaratives are how expensive
software speaks.
